# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Shopify store owners and e-commerce operators, typically founder-operators or lean marketing teams at D2C brands. They're opening this page after hearing about Clickpost through the App Store or word of mouth. They're mid-funnel: already aware order editing exists, skeptical it will work for their store, and pressed for time. They'll skim fast and bail if the value isn't immediately legible.

## Product Purpose

Clickpost Order Editing & Upsell is a Shopify app that lets customers fix their own orders (address, items, contact, discount, cancellation) on the post-purchase page, with no support ticket. The landing page exists to turn a skeptical Shopify merchant into an install. Success means a merchant clicking "See it on my store" or navigating to the Shopify App Store listing.

## Brand Personality

Warm, approachable, growth-focused. The tone is a helpful operator who's already seen the ROI and wants you to see it too. Confident without bragging. Data-backed without being dry. Never corporate, never indie-hacker bare.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream + purple (Webflow/Framer template aesthetic, every other Shopify app)
- Over-animated startup (gradient mesh, particle blobs, scroll-jacking, transitions that distract)
- Heavy enterprise (SAP/Salesforce feature walls, dense grids, dense copy, no soul)
- Indie-hacker minimal (no personality, just bare utility with no craft)

## Design Principles

1. **ROI first, features second.** Every section answers "what does this do for my business?" before explaining how it works. Numbers and outcomes lead.
2. **Show the real product.** Demo with the actual app UI, not illustrations or mockups. Trust comes from seeing the real thing.
3. **Merchant empathy.** Frame everything through the shopper experience that removes load from the merchant's support team. Make them feel seen.
4. **Earned sophistication.** Polish and craft signal quality without showing off. Motion, color, and space should feel intentional, not decorative.
5. **Convert, don't educate.** This is a landing page, not a docs page. Every element exists to reduce friction toward the install CTA, not to explain every feature exhaustively.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Support reduced-motion preference. Contrast ratios on all body text and interactive elements.
